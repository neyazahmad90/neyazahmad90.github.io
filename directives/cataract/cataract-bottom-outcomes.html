3. OUTCOMES

Cataract treatment can dramatically improve your quality of life, because it restores you to a level of activity that often isn't possible with cataracts. Overall satisfaction with eyesight improvement is so high that many patients wish they’d had cataract surgery sooner.

After cataract treatment, you can look forward to:
  • Brighter colors immediately after surgery
  • Eyesight improvement including crisper, clearer images once you heal
  • Renewed confidence-day and night
  • Greater freedom to engage in a full range of activities
  • 50% improvement in safety while driving
Studies show IOL recipients have their vision fully restored to its pre-cataract state. If you choose specialized IOLs, your vision may be even better than it was before.
a. STANDARD/MONOFOCAL INTRAOCULAR LENSES
If you choose a standard IOL, chances are excellent your eyesight improvement will be fully restored(the quality of vision you experienced before cataracts).
  • Standard IOLs only provide clear vision at one distance-near or mid-range or distance.
  • You may need glasses or contacts.
  • For most patients, having clear distance vision is most important, and they use reading glasses to see close objects clearly, just as many people do before cataracts.
b. MULTIFOCAL INTRAOCULAR LENSES
A good way to reduce your dependence on glasses after surgery.
Multifocal IOLs have a very high success rate and give you a good chance of not needing glasses after cataract treatment surgery. A large majority of patients report enough eyesight improvement that they either never or only occasionally need to wear glasses after surgery.
c. TORIC INTRAOCULAR LENSES
If you have astigmatism in addition to cataracts, cataract treatment with a toric IOL can often address it, making your vision even clearer after surgery than it was before you developed cataracts. This can also reduce your dependence on glasses or contacts after surgery.
d. MONOVISION
Another way to reduce your dependence on glasses. With monovision, you can reduce your dependence on reading glasses or bifocals after cataract treatment surgery. Your surgeon will implant a monofocal IOL for distance vision in one eye, while implanting one for near vision in the other. With this technique, you can obtain eyesight improvement for both near and distance vision. If you use toric IOLs to achieve monovision, you can correct astigmatism at the same time.
